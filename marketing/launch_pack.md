### Post #1 — r/googlesheets
**Title:**
> I built a simple budget tracker in Google Sheets — here's the structure that finally made me stick with budgeting

**Body:**
> After failing with budgeting apps (subscriptions, sync issues, overload), I went back to a plain Google Sheet and it finally stuck. The structure that worked for me:
>
> 1. **One Income tab** — date, amount, source (dropdown). Nothing else.
> 2. **One Expenses tab** — date, item, category (dropdown), amount.
> 3. **A Dashboard** that does everything automatically: SUMPRODUCT by month, spending by category with SUMIFS, and a savings rate cell.
>
> The trick that changed everything: the dashboard compares "this month" automatically using `TEXT(TODAY(),"yyyy-mm")` so I never have to update anything — I just log expenses.
>
> Happy to share the exact formulas if anyone wants them. (I also sell a ready-made version on Etsy for less than a coffee, link in my profile if the mods allow it — but honestly you can build this yourself in an afternoon.)

### Post #2 — r/personalfinance (لا تحط روابط هنا أبداً — بس قيمة)
**Title:**
> The "3-tab rule" that made me actually track my spending after years of failing

**Body:**
> Every budgeting system I tried died within 2 weeks — too complicated. What finally worked was radical simplicity: 3 tabs in a spreadsheet.
>
> - Tab 1: money in (date, amount, where from)
> - Tab 2: money out (date, what, category, amount)
> - Tab 3: a dashboard I never touch — it calculates income vs spending this month, and my savings rate
>
> Two rules I follow: log expenses same-day (takes 10 seconds on my phone), and review the dashboard every Sunday for 2 minutes.
>
> Month 1 result: found €140/month going to subscriptions I forgot about. The system doesn't matter — seeing the number does.
